# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small business owners running their own social media presence. They open this tool to check what's going out, approve posts, and monitor campaign health — not to configure pipelines. They are not technical. They are time-poor. They want to feel in control without needing to understand the machinery underneath.

## Product Purpose

Aladdyn Social automates the full social media content cycle: strategy generation, post writing, image creation, scheduling, and publishing to Instagram and LinkedIn. The business owner sets up a campaign once and the system handles the rest. The dashboard is where they review what the AI produced, approve it, and watch it go live.

## Brand Personality

Calm. Professional. Creative.

The tool should feel like a trusted operator — quiet confidence, not flashy. It does serious work and the interface reflects that. No hype, no noise. When something needs attention it surfaces clearly; when everything is running it stays out of the way.

## Anti-references

- Loud SaaS dashboards with gradient hero metrics and neon accent overload (Linear's calm is right; Notion's density is wrong for this context)
- Generic dark-mode tool aesthetic: purple/blue gradient everything, glassmorphism cards stacked on glassmorphism cards
- Marketing-page energy applied to app UI: big hero numbers, animated counters, "🚀 Supercharge your growth" copy
- Cluttered admin panels where every action competes for attention

## Design Principles

1. **Surface what needs action, hide what doesn't.** The dashboard's job is to tell the owner what requires their attention today. Everything else is secondary.
2. **Earned complexity.** Dev mode exists for power users. User mode should feel like a finished product, not a diagnostic console.
3. **Confidence through clarity.** The AI is doing complex work. The UI should make that feel reliable and legible, not impressive or overwhelming.
4. **One primary action per view.** Each screen has a clear next step. Competing CTAs create paralysis.
5. **Prose over data dumps.** The LLM summary, the campaign description, the upcoming post list — these should read like a briefing, not a spreadsheet.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast. Interactive elements ≥3:1. Reduced motion respected. No color-only status signalling — always pair color with icon or label.
